# Nexphoria Compliance Word-Flag List

Version: 1.0 | Effective: 2026-05-30 | Owner: Sentinel (Security & Compliance Officer)

Any output containing a flagged term MUST be routed to Sentinel for compliance review
before it is published, sent externally, or committed to a live environment.

---

## TIER 1 — Hard Block (Prohibited — never publish without Sentinel sign-off)

These terms constitute potential FDA/FTC violations if used in product context.

### Medical Claims
- cure, cures, cured, curative
- treat, treats, treating, treatment, treating disease
- diagnose, diagnosis, diagnostic
- prevent, prevents, preventing, prevention (when applied to disease)
- heal, heals, healing, healed (in medical context)
- therapy, therapeutic (when implying approved use)
- medicine, medication, drug (when referring to peptide products as such)
- clinically proven, clinically tested, clinically validated
- FDA approved, FDA cleared, FDA authorized
- doctor recommended, physician recommended, medical grade

### Human Use / Personal Outcome Guarantees
- safe for human consumption
- safe for injection in humans
- guaranteed results, guaranteed to work
- will lose weight, will build muscle, will increase
- cures your, treats your, heals your
- you will feel, you will see results
- proven to (+ outcome)

### Specific Condition Claims (in product context)
- cancer, tumor, oncology (as treatment claims)
- diabetes, diabetic, insulin resistance (as treatment claims)
- Alzheimer's, dementia, cognitive decline (as treatment claims)
- depression, anxiety, PTSD (as treatment claims)
- HIV, AIDS, autoimmune (as treatment claims)
- heart disease, cardiovascular disease (as treatment claims)
- obesity treatment, weight loss treatment

## TIER 2 — Caution (Require Sentinel review before publishing)

These terms are not inherently prohibited but require careful context review.

### Efficacy Language (without substantiation)
- proven, proof, evidence-based (when not citing peer-reviewed source)
- studies show, research shows (without citation)
- scientifically backed, science-backed (without citation)
- effective for, effective against
- works for, works on
- best, most powerful, strongest (superlative claims without substantiation)
- number one, #1

### Regulatory Adjacency
- peptide therapy
- hormone, hormonal
- anti-aging treatment
- longevity treatment
- bioidentical
- off-label
- compounded, compounding pharmacy

### Audience / Targeting
- for patients
- for sick people
- for those suffering from
- as a supplement to your medication
- instead of your medication
- replace your prescription

### Personal Testimonial Triggers (if generated, not curated)
- I lost X pounds
- my doctor said
- after using this, my [condition]
- cured my
- finally found something that

## TIER 3 — Flag for Logging (Log occurrence, notify Sentinel, may proceed with edit)

These are soft flags that should be reviewed and ideally rephrased.

- anti-aging (preferred: longevity research, healthy aging)
- fat loss (preferred: body composition)
- muscle growth (preferred: lean mass support in research context)
- weight loss (in product copy — flag and review)
- boost, boosts, boosting (with health claims)
- enhance, enhances (with unsubstantiated health claims)
- repair, repairs (in medical claim context)
- regenerate, regeneration (in medical claim context)
- anti-inflammatory (as product claim without citation)
- performance enhancement (implied PED context)

## Escalation Protocol

When a flagged term is detected:

1. **STOP** — do not publish, commit to production, or send externally.
2. **Log** the flagged term(s), the content block, and the output context.
3. **Create a Paperclip issue** assigned to Sentinel with:
   - Title: `[COMPLIANCE REVIEW] <agent name> — <brief description>`
   - Description: the flagged excerpt, term(s) triggered, and proposed fix
   - Priority: high (Tier 1) or medium (Tier 2/3)
   - Label/tag: `compliance`
4. **Await Sentinel's decision** before proceeding.
5. If Sentinel approves with edits, apply edits and republish.
6. If Sentinel rejects, discard or fully rewrite the flagged content.

## Scope

This list applies to ALL output generated by content agents including but not limited to:
- Blog articles and journal posts
- Product descriptions and copy
- Email marketing content
- Social media posts and captions
- Ad copy (Google, Meta, etc.)
- Customer support scripts and canned responses
- Analytics reports shared externally
- Documentation published publicly

It does NOT apply to:
- Internal agent-to-agent messages (Paperclip comments)
- Private run logs and dev notes
- Code (unless it contains user-facing copy strings)
