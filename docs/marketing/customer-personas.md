# Nexphoria Customer Personas
> Research peptide brand | 3 core buyer archetypes | June 2026

---

## Overview

Nexphoria's customer base clusters into three distinct archetypes based on primary research motivation, purchase driver, and content consumption behavior. These personas inform ad targeting, email segmentation, copy tone, and product prioritization.

---

## Persona 1: "The Methodical Researcher" — Marcus

**Tagline:** *"I need documentation I can actually cite."*

### Demographics
- **Age:** 35–55
- **Background:** Biomedical researcher, physician (often functional/integrative), PhD scientist in adjacent field, or sophisticated self-researcher with academic literacy
- **Income:** $80K–$200K
- **Location:** Primarily US metropolitan areas; university towns, research hubs
- **Education:** Graduate degree (common)

### Psychographics
- High skepticism toward vendor marketing; responds to evidence
- Reads actual studies, not summaries
- Values intellectual honesty over enthusiasm
- Distrust of unverifiable claims; will cross-reference COAs against database
- Methodical decision-making: researches extensively before first purchase, loyal once trust is established
- Finds most peptide vendor content superficial and frustrating

### Primary Research Focus
- Mechanism validation for specific compounds
- Protocol design (dosing, timing, administration route)
- Compound purity verification for data integrity
- Comparing research on combination protocols (stacks)

### Purchase Behavior
- High-value orders (often $200–$500+)
- Researches 2–4 weeks before first purchase
- Purchases based on documented evidence, not testimonials
- Becomes repeat customer; high LTV
- Likely to refer colleagues once trust established

### Key Pain Points
- Can't find COAs from most vendors that pass verification
- Vendor blogs are written for consumers, not researchers
- Purity inconsistencies between batches corrupt study data
- Hard to find honest discussion of evidence limitations

### Content That Converts
- Deep-dive mechanism articles with citations
- Third-party COA walkthrough
- Batch consistency documentation
- Honest "limitations of the evidence" framing
- Compound comparison guides with evidence-tier ratings

### Marketing Channel
- Google Search (research-query intent)
- Blog SEO (informational longevity/peptide queries)
- Reddit (r/nootropics, r/PeptideResearch — lurker/occasional commenter)
- Email (high engagement with educational content)

### Message That Resonates
*"Every COA third-party tested. Every lot publicly posted. Because your data is only as good as your compound."*

## Persona 2: "The Longevity Optimizer" — Elena

**Tagline:** *"I want to age better. I want the real science, not the supplement hype."*

### Demographics
- **Age:** 40–65
- **Background:** High-achieving professional (tech, finance, law, medicine), entrepreneur, or early retiree with disposable income and health-optimization mindset
- **Income:** $150K–$500K+
- **Location:** Major metros (NYC, LA, Miami, SF, Austin); also affluent suburban
- **Education:** Often highly educated; may not have formal science background but is highly intelligent and research-literate

### Psychographics
- Follows longevity science through Bryan Johnson, Peter Attia, David Sinclair content
- Tracks biomarkers (HRV, biological age tests, blood panels)
- Has already done: fasting protocols, zone 2 training, sleep optimization, NAD+ supplementation
- Views peptide research as the "next level" of evidence-based longevity intervention
- Willing to pay premium for quality; has been burned by supplement BS before
- Community-oriented: shares protocols with similar-minded peers

### Primary Research Focus
- Longevity and anti-aging compounds (Epithalon, GHK-Cu, NAD+, MOTS-c)
- GH axis optimization (CJC-1295, Ipamorelin, Sermorelin)
- Cognitive maintenance (Semax, Selank)
- Stack protocols combining multiple targets

### Purchase Behavior
- First purchase often $150–$300 (longevity bundle or stack kit)
- Becomes high-LTV subscriber if first experience is positive
- Likely to purchase multiple compounds for a stack protocol
- Responsive to subscription / auto-ship for convenience
- May purchase for a biohacker "protocol" — buys everything at once

### Key Pain Points
- Can't tell real research from vendor hype
- Doesn't want to wade through 30 websites with contradictory information
- Wants to understand the stack rationale, not just a product list
- Concerned about getting "fake" or low-purity compounds

### Content That Converts
- "2026 Longevity Stack Guide" type content
- Compound comparison articles (NMN vs NAD+, Epithalon vs TA-65)
- Curated "starter protocol" recommendations
- Evidence quality ratings ("Tier 1: Human RCT / Tier 2: Animal only")
- Celebrity/researcher association (Sinclair, Attia name-drops in context)

### Marketing Channel
- Instagram (follows longevity/biohacking accounts)
- Google Search (longevity-specific queries)
- YouTube (long-form health content consumption)
- Email (will subscribe for a protocol guide gated resource)
- Podcast advertising (Peter Attia, Huberman, Bryan Johnson adjacent)

### Message That Resonates
*"The 2026 longevity stack researchers are studying. HPLC-verified, US-manufactured, evidence-referenced. nexphoria.com"*

## Persona 3: "The Recovery-Focused Researcher" — Jake

**Tagline:** *"I'm serious about recovery. I want what actually works, with real purity data behind it."*

### Demographics
- **Age:** 28–45
- **Background:** Competitive athlete (CrossFit, powerlifting, BJJ, endurance), former military/law enforcement, physical therapist or sports medicine professional, fitness coach, or serious recreational athlete
- **Income:** $60K–$150K
- **Location:** Nationwide; strong in fitness-dense metros and suburban areas
- **Education:** Mixed; may not have formal science background but has high health literacy and is motivated to self-educate

### Psychographics
- Pragmatic: wants results, not theory
- Highly tolerant of nuance — understands "research only" framing and respects it
- Active in fitness communities (Reddit, Discord, Instagram fitness)
- Familiar with BPC-157 and TB-500 from community recommendations before finding Nexphoria
- Skeptical of over-the-counter supplements; has "been there, done that"
- Values peer recommendations and community proof over official marketing

### Primary Research Focus
- Injury recovery and tissue repair (BPC-157, TB-500, GHK-Cu)
- GH axis / body composition (CJC-1295, Ipamorelin, MK-677)
- Connective tissue health
- Recovery optimization stacks

### Purchase Behavior
- Initial purchase often focused on one specific compound ($80–$150)
- Often comes in through search ("BPC-157 for sale") or community referral
- Second purchase usually within 30 days if first experience is positive
- Responds to bundle pricing and stack kits
- Less brand-loyal than Marcus/Elena initially; converts to loyal through quality experience

### Key Pain Points
- Hard to find trustworthy source — lots of scammy vendors
- Wants to know COA is real, not just a PDF
- Shipping speed matters (injury context — can't wait 2 weeks)
- Needs basic protocol guidance alongside the product

### Content That Converts
- "BPC-157 vs TB-500 — which one do you need?" type articles
- Reconstitution and storage guides (practical how-to)
- Community-proof language (researchers and serious users, not celebrity testimonials)
- Fast shipping callouts
- Stack recommendations for specific goals ("recovery stack," "GH optimization stack")

### Marketing Channel
- Google Search (high-intent product queries: "buy bpc-157," "tb-500 for sale")
- Reddit (r/PeptideResearch, r/nootropics, fitness subreddits)
- Instagram (fitness community)
- YouTube (peptide guides, fitness recovery content)

### Message That Resonates
*"BPC-157, TB-500, and the full recovery stack — HPLC-verified, ships cold, COA included. nexphoria.com"*
