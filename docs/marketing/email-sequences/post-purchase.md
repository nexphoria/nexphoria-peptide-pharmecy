**Storage Guide for {{ item.product_name }}**

Proper storage is the single most important factor in maintaining compound integrity between receipt and use.

- **Lyophilized (powder) form:** Store at -20°C. Stable for 24+ months when kept dry and sealed. Avoid freeze-thaw cycling.
- **Once reconstituted:** Store at 4°C (refrigerator). Use within 4–6 weeks. Do not refreeze.
- **Reconstitution:** Use bacteriostatic water (BW) for peptides intended for extended use. Sterile water is acceptable for immediate use.
- **Light exposure:** Keep vials in the dark. UV degradation is a real factor for sensitive compounds.

Your shipment will arrive in insulated cold-chain packaging. Upon receipt, transfer vials to appropriate storage immediately.

**Protocol Notes — {{ item.product_name }}**

The following reflects ranges and methodologies observed in published preclinical studies. These are not instructions for human use.

**Reconstitution:**
Most lyophilized peptides reconstitute readily in bacteriostatic water. A standard approach is to introduce the solvent slowly against the vial wall, avoiding direct injection onto the powder. Swirl gently — do not vortex.

**Concentration:**
Common reconstitution concentrations in published studies range from 1–5 mg/mL depending on compound and application. Prepare no more volume than will be used within the stability window for your storage conditions.

**Dosing Ranges (animal model reference):**
Published rat model studies on BPC-157 typically range from 10–100 mcg/kg. NAD+ precursor studies vary substantially by route and model. Always reference the specific study methodology relevant to your research design.

**Controls:**
Saline or vehicle-matched controls are standard in published peptide research. Document your batch lot number (from the COA) for reproducibility.
