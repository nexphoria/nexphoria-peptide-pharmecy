## Email 2 — Send +2 Days

**Subject:** How we test every batch
**Preview Text:** HPLC and LAL testing — what they measure and why it matters.

---

**Body:**

Before any compound leaves our facility, it passes two independent tests. Here's what those tests actually measure — and why both matter to your research.

**HPLC (High-Performance Liquid Chromatography)**
HPLC separates the components of a sample and measures their relative concentrations. For peptides, it tells us the percentage of the target compound versus any impurities or degradation products. Our specification: ≥99% purity. If a batch doesn't meet that threshold, it doesn't ship.

**LAL (Limulus Amebocyte Lysate) Testing**
HPLC confirms purity. LAL testing confirms safety at the bench — it detects bacterial endotoxins that can remain even in chemically pure samples. Endotoxin contamination is a common source of confounded results in peptide research. Every Nexphoria batch is LAL-tested and must pass endotoxin limits before release.

Both tests are performed per lot, and the results are available to you.
