### WHAT MAKES A VENDOR TRUSTWORTHY?

**1. HPLC Purity Documentation**
Any reputable vendor publishes HPLC data showing purity levels. The standard is ≥98%; premium vendors hold ≥99%. HPLC measures compound purity but doesn't confirm identity — for that you need mass spectrometry.

**2. Mass Spectrometry (MS) Identity Verification**
MS confirms the compound is what it says it is. Without MS, you know it's pure but not necessarily pure [correct compound]. The top-tier vendors run both.

**3. Publicly Verifiable COAs**
The COA needs to be traceable to a real lab batch. If a vendor won't tell you which testing lab ran the analysis, or if the COA lacks a batch/lot number that can be independently verified with the lab, the document is effectively unverifiable.

**4. Cold-Chain Shipping**
Lyophilized peptides are stable at room temperature for limited periods — but shipping thermal stress accelerates degradation. Best practice is cold-pack + insulated packaging. Not all vendors do this standard.

**5. US Manufacturing vs. International**
US-manufactured compounds don't face customs seizure risk and operate under domestic legal frameworks. International vendors (Swiss Chems and others) introduce shipping delays and customs uncertainty.
