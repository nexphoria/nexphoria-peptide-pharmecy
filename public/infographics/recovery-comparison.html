Mechanism of Action
BPC-157
VEGF / NO / GH-R Pathway
1
Upregulates VEGF expression → promotes angiogenesis
2
Modulates NO (nitric oxide) system → vasodilation, cytoprotection
3
Interacts with GH receptor → activates Akt/mTOR pathway
4
Reduces oxidative stress markers in preclinical tissue models
Unique Property
Exceptional plasma stability — resistant to degradation in gastric acid and intestinal secretions. Oral route viable for GI-targeted research.
TB-500
Actin / MMP / Wnt Pathway
1
Sequesters G-actin via LKKTETQ domain → regulates actin dynamics
2
Activates MMP expression → promotes ECM remodeling
3
Stimulates keratinocyte migration → wound healing models
4
Modulates Wnt signaling → reduces TNF-α / IL-6 in inflammation models
Unique Property
Largest molecule in category (4,963 g/mol). Ubiquitous in virtually all mammalian cells — the LKKTETQ fragment retains full biological activity of full-length Tβ4.
GHK-Cu
Copper / TGF-β / SOD Pathway
1
Cu²⁺ complex stimulates collagen and GAG synthesis in fibroblasts
2
Activates TGF-β signaling → ECM remodeling and skin architecture
3
Upregulates SOD and catalase → antioxidant defense in vitro
4
Modulates gene expression — activates ~31 repair genes in studies
Unique Property
Smallest research compound in category (MW 340 g/mol). Topical route viable. Endogenous human plasma peptide — highest physiological relevance of the three.

Research Context Highlights
BPC-157
Broadest tissue-repair literature
Most extensively published preclinical peptide in the recovery category. Research spans tendon/ligament, gastric epithelium, CNS, and vascular tissue across multiple animal models. Exceptional plasma stability enables viable oral route studies unique among tissue-repair peptides.
TB-500
Unique actin-axis mechanism
Only compound in the category acting primarily on the actin cytoskeleton. G-actin sequestration via LKKTETQ is a mechanistically distinct target not addressed by BPC-157 or GHK-Cu. Strong cardiac ischemia-reperfusion and neovascularization literature base.
GHK-Cu
Smallest, most ECM-specific
Tripeptide copper complex at 340 g/mol — smallest molecule in recovery category. Endogenous human plasma peptide with documented collagen/elastin/GAG upregulation in fibroblast models. Topical viability and gene expression modulation (~31 repair genes) differentiate it from larger peptide competitors.
